Overview

agristoolkit is a Stata package developed to streamline the production, management, and dissemination of statistical outputs derived from complex survey data. It enables users to generate standardized, long-format multi-dimensional tables of indicators computed with parameters such as mean, total, ratio, and median while properly accounting for survey design features (weights, stratification, and clustering) through the genmdt function.

In addition, the package provides tools to transform these machine-readable datasets into well-structured, publication-ready tables via the tab_from_mdt function, facilitating their direct integration into analytical reports, briefs, and other dissemination products.

A key feature of agristoolkit is its support for statistical quality assurance. The package incorporates mechanisms to monitor and evaluate the reliability of estimates based on criteria such as the number of sample observations, standard errors, and coefficients of variation. These metrics can be used to guide decisions on whether indicators should be published, flagged, or masked, ensuring that both machine-readable outputs and publication-ready tables meet predefined quality standards.

The package was designed to address a common dual need in statistical production systems: (i) the creation of detailed, structured datasets suitable for statistical databases and data portals, and (ii) the generation of simplified, human-readable tables for reporting and communication purposes.

By linking both outputs to a single underlying data structure, agristoolkit ensures full consistency, reproducibility, and efficiency across data dissemination workflows, reducing duplication of effort and minimizing the risk of discrepancies between published results and underlying data systems.

Installation

Install directly from GitHub or your repository:

net install agristoolkit, from("https://raw.githubusercontent.com/amsata/agristoolkit/main/") replace

Main Features

  • Generation of machine-readable multi-dimensional tables (genmdt [varlist][,options]): agristoolkit enables the production of standardized, long-format tables of survey indicators (mean, total, ratio, median) that fully account for complex survey design. These structured outputs are optimized for integration into statistical databases and data portals, while ensuri ng consistency and reproducibility.

  • Production of publication-ready tables with quality control (tab_from_mdt): The package allows users to convert machine-readable outputs into clear, formatted tables for reports and briefs. It integrates statistical quality checks—based on sample size, standard errors, and coefficients of variation—to support decisions on publishing, flagging, or masking indicators, ensuring reliable and coherent dissemination.
